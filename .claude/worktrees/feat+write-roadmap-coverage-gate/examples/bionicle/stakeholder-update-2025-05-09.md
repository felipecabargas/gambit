## Risks & Blockers

- **Android mid-range performance not benchmarked:** The AR scanner launched on iOS and performed well, but Android mid-range devices (the majority of the addressable market) have not been stress-tested. The May 28 influencer campaign will drive a surge of Android installs. A crash or degraded experience at that moment would directly hit ratings and retention at the worst possible time.

---

## Decisions Needed from Leadership

**Go/no-go: Android benchmarking before May 28 influencer campaign**
Context: AR scanner performance on Android mid-range devices is untested. The campaign launches in 19 days and is expected to drive the single largest install spike since launch.
Options: (A) Prioritise Android benchmarking this sprint — pull one feature ticket to make room and confirm go/no-go by May 19. (B) Proceed with the campaign without benchmarking and have a hotfix team on standby. (C) Delay the influencer campaign by 2 weeks to allow a full benchmarking and fix cycle.
Ask: Decision needed by May 14 so engineering can adjust Sprint 8 scope accordingly.

## Next Period Focus

Sprint 8 priority is multiplayer co-build (backend complete, frontend 40%) and, pending the decision above, Android AR benchmarking. Hitting KR1 (D7 retention 45%) likely requires multiplayer — it's the highest-engagement mechanic remaining on the roadmap.
