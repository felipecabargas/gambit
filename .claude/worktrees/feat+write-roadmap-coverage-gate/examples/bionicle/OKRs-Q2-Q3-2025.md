# OKRs: Q2–Q3 2025

**Strategy source:** STRATEGY.md
**Date drafted:** 2025-05-09
**Owner:** Bionicle Digital PM, LEGO Group

## Objective 1: Make every physical set the start of a deeper digital experience

**Strategy pillar:** Physical-digital loop is the product
**Why this quarter:** 67% of churn is caused by content exhaustion post-build. The AR scanner is live and loved. The work now is proving that each new set creates a reason to return — not just a reason to download.

### KR 1.1: Increase D7 retention from 41% to 45%
- **Baseline → Target:** 41% → 45%
- **Measurement:** D7 retention cohort, measured weekly in app analytics. Cohort defined as users who complete onboarding and open the app at least once on day 7.
- **Confidence:** Committed — trending at +8 points over the last two sprints. Multiplayer shipping in Sprint 8 provides the next structural push.

### KR 1.2: Maintain weekly AR scan rate above 50% of active users
- **Baseline → Target:** 62% (week 1 post-launch) → ≥50% sustained weekly average over the quarter
- **Measurement:** % of weekly active users who complete at least one mask scan per 7-day period.
- **Confidence:** Stretch — week-1 numbers reflect novelty. Sustained engagement beyond the initial scan is unproven; new set releases are the primary lever.

### KR 1.3: Reach 500k cumulative downloads by end of Q3
- **Baseline → Target:** TBD current total → 500,000
- **Measurement:** Combined App Store + Google Play installs, reported weekly.
- **Confidence:** Committed — this is a retailer contractual milestone. Missing it triggers shelf space renegotiation. It is treated as a floor, not a target.

## Objective 2: Turn the Bionicle community into the reason players open the app

**Strategy pillar:** Community and competition as the retention engine
**Why this quarter:** Multiplayer co-build ships in Sprint 8. The social layer goes from zero to one this quarter. Without it, D30 retention cannot reach competitive benchmarks regardless of content volume.

### KR 2.1: Increase D30 retention from baseline to 35%
- **Baseline → Target:** TBD — establish in week 1 of Q2 → 35% by end of Q3
- **Measurement:** D30 retention cohort in app analytics, same definition as D7 cohort. Baseline to be established in the first week of Q2 before multiplayer ships.
- **Confidence:** Stretch — 35% is the social game retention benchmark. Achieving it requires multiplayer to ship cleanly and drive habitual use. No prior data point to extrapolate from.

### KR 2.2: Get 25% of weekly active users into at least one multiplayer session per week
- **Baseline → Target:** 0% (feature not live) → 25%
- **Measurement:** % of WAU completing a multiplayer co-build session in a given 7-day window. Tracked from multiplayer launch date.
- **Confidence:** Stretch — multiplayer is the most-requested feature (top NPS ask), which is a positive signal, but first-session completion rate and return-to-multiplayer rate are unknown until the feature is live.

### KR 2.3: Drive weekly build challenge participation to 40% of active users
- **Baseline → Target:** TBD — establish at end of Sprint 7 (leaderboard just shipped) → 40%
- **Measurement:** % of WAU who submit at least one entry to the weekly build challenge.
- **Confidence:** Stretch — leaderboard shipped this sprint; baseline will be established in the first two weeks of Q2.

## Objective 3: Make creator content the most efficient path to a new Bionicle player

**Strategy pillar:** Creator partnerships as primary acquisition
**Why this quarter:** The influencer campaign launches May 28. App Store review analysis shows creator discovery already dominates organic acquisition. Formalising the creator program and measuring its efficiency this quarter determines whether it becomes the primary acquisition channel at scale or remains opportunistic.

### KR 3.1: Grow creator-attributed installs to 40% of all new installs by end of Q3
- **Baseline → Target:** TBD — establish formal attribution tracking in week 1 → 40% by end of Q3
- **Measurement:** Installs attributed to creator-specific tracking links or promo codes, as a percentage of total new installs in the period. Attribution window: 7 days from click/view.
- **Confidence:** Stretch — App Store review qualitative data suggests creator discovery is already significant, but formal attribution does not exist yet. The 40% target is directionally grounded but unvalidated.

### KR 3.2: Achieve creator channel CPI at least 50% lower than paid UA CPI
- **Baseline → Target:** Paid UA CPI TBD (establish in Q2) → creator CPI ≤50% of paid UA CPI
- **Measurement:** Cost per attributed install across creator program spend vs. paid UA spend, measured monthly.
- **Confidence:** Stretch — STRATEGY.md targets 60% lower CPI. 50% is the Committed threshold, 60% is the stretch within this KR.
