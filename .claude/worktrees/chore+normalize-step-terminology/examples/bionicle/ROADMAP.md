## Introduction

Bionicle Reborn is a connected toy franchise where physical sets unlock a living digital world. This roadmap executes the three-pillar strategy — deepening the physical-digital loop, building community as the retention engine, and establishing creator partnerships as the primary acquisition channel — in service of 45% D7 retention and 500k downloads by Q3 2025.

---

## Now — Q2 2025

> Committed work. The team is accountable for delivery this period.

### Theme: Multiplayer Foundation
**Outcome:** Players can build Toa together in real time, giving them a reason to open the app that exists independently of whether they own a new physical set. D30 retention has a social floor for the first time.
**Strategy pillar:** Community and competition as the retention engine
**KR:** D30 retention baseline established; multiplayer session rate tracking begins
**Status:** Committed — backend complete, frontend delivery in Sprint 8

### Theme: Android AR Parity
**Outcome:** The AR scanner works reliably across the Android mid-range device tier that represents the majority of the 8–14 addressable market. The May 28 influencer campaign launches to a product that works for every user who downloads it.
**Strategy pillar:** Physical-digital loop is the product
**KR:** D7 retention (KR 1.1); 500k downloads (KR 1.3)
**Status:** Committed — must complete before May 28 campaign launch
**Hard deadline:** May 19 (go/no-go decision required by this date)

### Theme: Creator Program Launch
**Outcome:** A structured cohort of YouTube and TikTok Bionicle creators amplifies the May 28 campaign with trackable links, driving the first formal measurement of creator-attributed install share. Creator channel CPI is established as a baseline against paid UA.
**Strategy pillar:** Creator partnerships as primary acquisition
**KR:** Creator-attributed install share (KR 3.1); creator CPI vs paid UA (KR 3.2)
**Status:** Committed — campaign date May 28 is fixed

## Next — Q3–Q4 2025

> Directional. Strong intent; details will be refined as Q2 work completes.

### Theme: Set-Launch as Content Event
**Outcome:** Each new physical set release is coordinated with an in-app content drop — new lore region, new challenge arc, new mask abilities — so that the week a set hits retail is also the highest-engagement week in the app. The physical-digital loop becomes bidirectional: app engagement drives set purchase intent, not just the reverse.
**Strategy pillar:** Physical-digital loop is the product
**KR:** Weekly AR scan rate sustained ≥50% (KR 1.2); D7 retention maintained at 45%+
**Status:** Directional
**Depends on:** Creator Program Launch (Now); next set wave release cadence confirmed with retail

### Theme: Community Events
**Outcome:** Monthly community events — Mask of Light tournaments, Toa build-offs, lore voting — give players a recurring reason to return between set launches. Event participation becomes a leading indicator of D30 retention.
**Strategy pillar:** Community and competition as the retention engine
**KR:** D30 retention to 35% (KR 2.1); build challenge participation to 40% WAU (KR 2.3)
**Status:** Directional
**Depends on:** Multiplayer Foundation (Now); COPPA legal review complete

### Theme: User-Generated Lore
**Outcome:** Fans can contribute to the Bionicle Reborn canon through moderated story fragments, community-voted lore decisions, and UGC mask lore. The world expands between official set releases, driven by the community rather than the content team alone.
**Strategy pillar:** Community and competition as the retention engine
**KR:** D30 retention (KR 2.1); multiplayer session rate (KR 2.2)
**Status:** Directional
**Depends on:** Community Events (Next); IP moderation guidelines published; COPPA review complete

### Theme: Creator Exclusives & Expansion
**Outcome:** Top-performing creators from the May 28 cohort receive early access to unreleased content and exclusive in-app items for their audiences. Creator-attributed install share grows toward the 40% target as the program expands from launch cohort to ongoing partnership.
**Strategy pillar:** Creator partnerships as primary acquisition
**KR:** Creator-attributed install share to 40% (KR 3.1); creator CPI vs paid UA (KR 3.2)
**Status:** Directional
**Depends on:** Creator Program Launch (Now) — performance data from Q2 determines expansion criteria

## Later — 2026

> Exploratory. Strategic intent established; not yet scoped or resourced.

### Theme: Seasonal Narrative Arcs
**Outcome:** The Bionicle Reborn story advances on a seasonal cadence — major canon events, villain reveals, arc resolutions — independent of physical set releases. The app becomes a living narrative platform that retains players across years, not just across set launches.
**Strategy pillar:** Physical-digital loop is the product; Community as retention engine
**Status:** Directional
**Depends on:** Set-Launch as Content Event (Next); UGC Lore (Next) — community content infrastructure must exist before editorial seasonal arcs can scale

### Theme: Cross-Set Mechanics
**Outcome:** Owning multiple sets unlocks experiences unavailable to single-set owners — co-op challenges requiring two different Toa types, world regions that unlock only when specific mask combinations are scanned. The app rewards collection and creates social dynamics around set ownership.
**Strategy pillar:** Physical-digital loop is the product
**Status:** Directional
**Depends on:** Set-Launch as Content Event (Next); multiplayer infrastructure (Now)

### Theme: Physical-to-Physical Multiplayer
**Outcome:** Two players with different physical sets can unlock a co-op experience neither can access alone — their Toa types and mask combinations determine the challenge. The physical sets become social objects, not just individual collectibles.
**Strategy pillar:** Community and competition as the retention engine
**Status:** Directional — exploratory, dependent on multiplayer retention data from Q3–Q4 validating the investment thesis

## Explicitly Not Doing

| Area | Reason |
|---|---|
| Paid UA as primary acquisition channel | Creator partnerships are delivering lower CPI with higher intent users. Paid UA spend is maintained as a baseline but will not be scaled — budget is better deployed in the creator program until CPI data proves otherwise. |
| Web or desktop companion app | ARKit/ARCore requires mobile hardware. A web experience cannot deliver the scanner mechanic that is the product's core engagement driver. Browser support would require a fundamentally different product. |
| Cross-franchise IP crossovers (e.g. LEGO City, Technic) | Bionicle's community is defined by canon fidelity. Crossovers with non-Bionicle IP risk alienating the nostalgic adult segment and diluting lore integrity before the world is established. Revisit in 2027 if community sentiment data supports it. |
| Standalone app mode (no physical set required) | The physical-digital loop is the strategic bet. A standalone digital experience competes with games and has no retail distribution advantage. Decoupling the app from the physical product removes the reason for LEGO's retail partners to co-market it. |
